# Source-photo capture guide for the Asset Pack

Status: wontfix (folded into 07-onboard-asset-pack — content absorbed there, not rejected; do not grab this ticket separately)

## Problem

Prototype evidence (2026-07-06): photo quality is the ceiling on output quality, and
the two shoots each got half of it right —

- Round A photos: great exaggerated expressions (grimace, shock) but flat indoor
  lighting → subject looked pasted onto scenes.
- Warm reshoot (round D): clean warm directional light but neutral expressions →
  editorial styles worked, high-energy wordings had nothing to pair with.

A winning Asset Pack needs BOTH, and clients will make the same mistakes unless told
exactly what to shoot.

## Wanted

A one-page capture guide (checklist + example shots) sent to every creator at
onboarding:

- Warm, directional key light (window light or a single lamp at ~45°), no flat
  overhead lighting.
- 10-20 shots covering an expression range: shock, grimace/disgust, joy, curiosity,
  pointing (both hands), arms crossed, neutral.
- Camera at eye level, subject fills frame chest-up, plain background (rembg-friendly).
- No mirrored/selfie-flipped captures if clothing has text/logos (or shoot both ways).
- Plain-colored clothing without large logos preferred; avoid colors that clash with
  the channel's palette.
- Phone camera is fine; avoid beauty filters (identity preservation, ADR-0001).

Also becomes the acceptance checklist the operator runs when an Asset Pack arrives
(reject/re-request photos that fail it).
