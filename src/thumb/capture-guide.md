# Source-photo capture guide

Send this page to every Creator at onboarding. The same checklist below is what
`thumb onboard` evaluates when the photos arrive — photos that fail it get
rejected or re-requested before they become the ceiling on every Thumbnail.

## Why both light AND expressions

A winning Asset Pack needs warm directional light **and** exaggerated
expressions in the same shoot. One without the other produces thumbnails that
either look pasted-on (good faces, flat light) or have nothing to pair with
high-energy wordings (good light, neutral faces).

## The checklist

- [check: lighting = directional] Warm, directional key light — window light or
  a single lamp at roughly 45°. No flat overhead lighting.
- [check: background = plain] Plain background behind you (a bare wall is
  perfect) so the cutout comes out clean.
- [check: filtered = false] No beauty filters or AI enhancement — your real
  face must stay your real face.
- [check-set: expression-variety >= 3] Cover an expression range across the
  set: shock, grimace/disgust, joy, curiosity — plus pointing (both hands),
  arms crossed, and neutral.
- [check-set: photo-count 10-20] Send 10–20 shots total so every Order has
  options.

## Shooting tips

- Camera at eye level, you fill the frame chest-up.
- Phone camera is fine.
- Plain-colored clothing without large logos. If clothing has text, shoot both
  ways (facing left and right) so we never need to mirror it.
- Do each expression bigger than feels natural — thumbnails are seen tiny.
